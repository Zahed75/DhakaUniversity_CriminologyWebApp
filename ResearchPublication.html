Research & Publication

Home Publication Details.

Publications:

Causes behind Radicalization & Counter Approach (in collaboration with DMP)


The project was conducted in collaboration with the Dhaka Metropolitan Police. The objective of the study was to explore the various causative and facilitative factors of radicalization and eventually provide some strategic counter approach recommendations. The methodology included content analysis, opinion survey conducted on public and private university students in Bangladesh and in-depth interviews of CTTU officials, experts and academicians.

Nirbaan

Nirbaan is a publication by the students of the Department of Criminology. The first issue was published in November of 2019.The magazine is a collective effort by students to disseminate the ideas of Criminology to the multitude. The first issue focuses on Violent crimes, and sheds light on the contemporary social and global issues of the world of crimes via a criminological perspective. Besides that, the analytical publication is accompanied with fiction, riddles, crosswords, and movie reviews to accomplish the complete package.

Ongoing Research Projects:

The Department of Criminology encourages its students to think critically and participate in various research projects on contemporary social and criminal issues. Currently, the department is doing a research project with the Police Headquarters on Sensational Death Cases and Blowback of Police Image. The previous research projects are:

  1. • Community Policing and Countering Extremism in Bangladesh

    With the collaboration of the Asia Foundation, the purpose of the project was to assess the community policing scenario in Bangladesh including its areas of jurisdictions and challenges faced during the process. It also aimed in identifying the role and scope of community policing in countering violent extremism in the country.

  2. • Problems and Limitations in Criminal Prosecution and Case Management

    In collaboration with CID Bangladesh, this research project used both quantitative and qualitative approaches to gather relevant information from the range of stakeholders including victims, Police officials, Court police, Public Prosecutor, NGO and GoB representatives and investigated the existing challenges in criminal case prosecution and management process.

Mission:
The department aims to study crime and criminal behavior from a scientific perspective, while also critically analyzing such events in the societal context of Bangladesh. Criminology in a developing country like Bangladesh requires the discipline to be empirical, data-driven, and policy relevant. The department of Criminology intends to provide its students with knowledge and tools that will ignite critical thinking and enable them to develop new crime theories, develop and modernize surveys on criminal victimization, analyze crime trends, understand the nature of implementation of some of the existing laws, application of the due process of law in law enforcement and the judiciary development and the methods and challenges of crime preventions. In accomplishing the goal, the department has introduced many contemporary courses to investigate crime cause and prevent criminal activities in Bangladesh.
Alumni Association:
The Department of Criminology established Dhaka University Criminology Alumnae Association (DUCAA) in 2018, comprising of 39 committee members. Monirul Islam, the chief of Counter Terrorism Unit of Bangladesh has been elected as the president of DUCAA, alongside Supreme Courte attorney Nazneen Sultana Sukhee as the General Secretary, and the deputy commissioner of the Counter Terrorism Unit Abdul Mannan as the organizing secretory
Facilities for Students:
The Department of Criminology offers a plethora of facilities for its students. The department’s classroom is equipped with various technologically advanced tools such as a smart board, digital screen, and a projector to ensure an advanced and engaging learning environment. The air-conditioned classroom can host about 150 students. The department also has recently set up a computer lab with a lab technician and 24 fully functioning computers and hi-speed internet facilities. The department also has established seminar library this year where the students will have access to a significant number of books of related field of criminology. The department aims to establish its crime lab for providing for practical lessons including criminal investigation, forensic science, digital forensics, cybercrime, cybersecurity practice and management, etc. and it is in progress. The department has developed partnerships for internship opportunities with SERAC Bangladesh, Cyber Crime Awareness Foundation and Jatiyo Mohila Parishad for the departmental students. The department initiated an online guidance and mental support program to the students for both academic and personal issues with the assistance of the Department of Educational and Counselling Psychology for a period of three months.
Extra-Curricular Activities
Arranging Colloquium: The Department of Criminology arranges monthly colloquium where public intellectuals and scholars in different fields lecture on contemporary issues. These colloquiums aim to provoke students’ intellectual capabilities, to think about the contemporary world and changes, to understand how people around the world as well as Bangladesh are coping and reacting with these changes and in this context how the nature of crime is changing and what would be the proper mechanism of preventing crime etc. For example, in March 2020, the Department of Criminology celebrated International Women’s Day and organized a workshop on “Violence against Women’ and documentary screening in collaboration with Bangadesh Mohila Samity. Very recently, the department hosts a virtual talk on Perspectives of Crime and Crime Prevention in the age of Covid 19". Professor Dr Zia Rahman and Monirul Islam, DIG, Bangladesh Police were the speakers.

International Conference:

The department organized its first international conference on Terrorism in the Wave of Islamic States in 2017 and Rohingya: Politics, Ethnic Cleansing and Uncertainty in 2018. The department received a numerous number of papers on this topic from various participants from all over the world. The best papers were presented in various sessions during the conference. In considering the three landmark events of the country, i.e., the golden jubilee of independence of Bangladesh, centennial birth celebration of the Father of Nation Bangabandhu Seikh Mujibar Rahman and the Celebration of DU Centenary, the Department of Criminology, University of Dhaka in collaboration with Ryukoku University, Japan and National Forensic Sciences University, will organize an international conference on Criminology and Forensic Science in the Global South from 8 to 9 August 2021.


Co-curricular Activities:

The Department of Criminology encourages the students to participate in different games and sports events (i.e., football, cricket, handball etc). Hence, the students regularly participate in “Inter Department Football Tournament” and “Inter Department Cricket Tournament”. In a similar fashion, the department encourages the cultural development of the students in the arena of literature, music, dance, debate etc. Simultaneously, as part of the commitment to protect environment, the Department organizes monthly Cleanliness Awareness Program to engage students in community works.
